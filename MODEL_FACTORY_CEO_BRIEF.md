# System Dynamics Studio — What the board gets

**One page for the CEO / board.**  
No technical jargon. What this approach does for you: **insight**, **speed**, **reliability**.

---

## What problem this solves

You don’t want one giant model that tries to do everything. You want a **repeatable way** to build small, focused models for different strategic questions—with as little friction as possible—so the board can explore options without waiting months or depending on a single expert.

---

## What you get

| Need | How the factory delivers |
|------|---------------------------|
| **Insight** | Each model answers one question (e.g. *“What happens to our long-term business if we heavily invest in lethal AI?”*). You see how key variables (backlash, regulation, reputation, contracts) interact over 5–10 years under different assumptions. Scenarios in minutes, not weeks. |
| **Speed** | Same process every time: agree a one-page **Model Brief** (question, scope, horizon), then the task force builds one small model. New question → new model in hours to days, not months. The same engine runs all models. |
| **Reliability** | Assumptions are explicit (stocks, flows, parameters). You can see where each relationship comes from (provenance). Models are editable and auditable—no black box. |

---

## How it works (no tech required to read this)

1. **One question, one model.** We never build a firm-wide mega-model. Each engagement = one strategic “what if” or “how does X interact with Y?”
2. **Model Brief = contract.** The board (or sponsor) fills a short template: question, who uses it, time horizon, what’s in scope, what’s out. That’s the only mandatory input. No long discovery workshops.
3. **Build from building blocks.** We reuse a small set of structures (pipeline, reputation/trust, capacity–demand, etc.) and adapt them to the question. That keeps models comparable and fast to build.
4. **Scenarios, not point forecasts.** Every run compares a base case with optimistic, pessimistic, and 1–2 policy levers (e.g. “+20% non-lethal share”, “stricter oversight”). You see ranges and trade-offs, not a single number.
5. **Transparent and updatable.** Every relationship is documented (source, type of loop, delay). Parameters can be tuned; structure can be adjusted. The “System Dynamics Studio” is the process plus the shared engine—not a single static spreadsheet.

---

## What we don’t promise

- **No perfect predictions.** The world is complex and uncertain. These models help you reason and compare options; they don’t replace judgment.
- **No single right answer.** What to include, how to interpret conflicting information, which horizon to use—those are human choices. The factory makes the *process* systematic; the *choices* stay with you.

---

## Bottom line

You get a **credible, repeatable way** to turn strategic questions into simple system-dynamics models that the board can understand and trust. Same approach for the next question—so over time you build a library of small models, not one unmaintainable monster.
